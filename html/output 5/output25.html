Research Objectives

1.) To compare the photosynthetic responses of Acer mono and Acer pseudosieboldianum seedlings under different light intensities (100%, 75%, 55%, and 20% of full light).

2.) To evaluate the growth performance of both species in terms of seedling height, basal stem diameter, leaf number, leaf area per plant, and total dry weight under varying light conditions.

3.) To determine the physiological adaptations of seedlings through measurements of antioxidant enzyme activities (SOD, POD, CAT), malondialdehyde (MDA) content, free proline, and soluble protein levels.

4.) To assess the overall adaptability and shade tolerance strategies of both maple species based on their photosynthetic, morphological, and physiological traits.

5.) To identify which species exhibits greater resilience and adaptability to light-induced stress.
