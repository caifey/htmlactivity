References

van Emmerik, Timm, et al. (2023). River plastic transport and deposition amplified by extreme flood. Nature Water. https://doi.org/10.1038/s44221-023-00092-7 This study provides observational evidence showing how floods dramatically increase plastic transport and accumulation in rivers and discusses the physical behavior of macroplastic during flood events.

Geng, Xiaolong, Michel C. Boufadel, and Edward P. Lopez. (2023). Modeling impacts of river hydrodynamics on fate and transport of microplastics in riverine environments. Marine Pollution Bulletin, . https://doi.org/10.1016/j.marpolbul.2023.115602 A numerical modeling study focusing on particle transport in river systems, providing insights into integrating hydrodynamics and particle transport.

Talavera, Allyza L., Lorrayne Faye M. Dalida, and Ma. Brida Lea D. Diola. (2024). Riverine macroplastic survey along the segments of Tullahan River in Metro Manila, Philippines. Frontiers in Environmental Science, https://doi.org/10.3389/fenvs.2024.1396525 A case study in Metro Manila that quantifies macroplastic distribution and flux in an urban river, useful for comparison with the Marikina River context.
